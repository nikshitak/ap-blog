AP Calculus AB and BC

AP Calculus AB will help you express problems graphically, numerically, analytically, and verbally. You'll learn about limits and continuity, differentiation, integration, and the applications of those. AP Calculus BC is the same as AB, but in addition to limits, differentiation, and integration, you'll learn about parametric equations, polar coordinates, vector-valued functions, as well as infinite sequences and series. The course itself wasn't that bad, but I did need to study a bit outside of class.

Difficulty: 4/5

The image will take you to the Calc BC overview since the course and exam description for both of the classes are the same and because Calc BC is just an extension of Calc AB.

AP Statistics

This class is good for anyone who's trying to find patterns in many different sets of data. AP Stats will help you collect, analyze, and draw conclusions from different sets of data. You'll learn about probabilistic reasoning to anticipate patterns in data and to determine the likelihood of reference errors, as well as deduce if different patterns in the data represent random variations or not. It is a lot of work class-wise; you'll get lots of homework and you'll need to write a lot to justify your conclusions. Therefore, my advice would be to just take it in college.

Difficulty: 4.5/5

AP Computer Science A

I enjoyed this class a lot. All the labs that we did were fun, and the new content built on the skills that we learned earlier in the course, reinforcing the knowledge. The language for this course is Java. In the class, you'll learn about variables; for, if, and while loops; iteration; arrays and ArrayLists; classes and inheritance; and recursion (probably the hardest unit in my opinion). This is probably the only AP class I actually enjoyed other than AP World History.

Difficulty: 1.5/5
